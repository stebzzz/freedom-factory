You are 6 years old. Rome, 94 BC.

The house smells like old marble and older debt.

Your family name is ancient. Julius Caesar.

It opens doors the way a drawn sword parts a crowd,

but there is no gold left behind those doors.

The name is the inheritance. The debt is real.

Your family lives crammed into the Sabura,

the wrong side of the city. loud, overcrowded,

knife sharp after dark.

From the upstairs window, you watch the senators pass in their litters.

White toggas, soft hands, rings on every finger.

You memorize every face.

You are 6 years old and you already understand one thing with absolute clarity.

A name without power is nothing more than noise.
